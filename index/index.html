About Me

I'm a first-year Ph.D. student in the Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University. My research interests are in theoretical computer science, and I'm lucky to be advised by Pravesh Kothari and Aayush Jain. I'm supported by an NSF GRFP fellowship. For my CV, see here, and for a list of my papers, see here.

In Spring 2022, I graduated magna cum laude from Harvard with an A.B. in Computer Science and Math. At Harvard, I was fortunate to be mentored by Madhu Sudan, and I was a proud resident of Pforzheimer House.

Research Overview

I have broad interests throughout complexity and algorithms. Recently, I've been thinking about average-case complexity and combinatorial optimization, with applications to cryptography, coding theory, and combinatorics. Related technical ideas are Sum-of-Squares relaxations, high-dimensional expanders, and various random models.

Specific Projects

I have ongoing interest in understanding approximations for CSPs in streaming models. I was introduced to the area during my undergrad by Santhoshini Velusamy and Madhu Sudan. With them and other wonderful collaborators I have several papers on the subject, appearing in APPROX 2021 and 2022, and in submission. This work was also the subject of my undergraduate thesis, which was awarded a Hoopes Prize.
