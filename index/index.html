About Me

I study computer science and math at Harvard University (Pforzheimer House '22). My research is in theoretical computer science. At Harvard, I am fortunate to be working at Harvard with Prof. Madhu Sudan on streaming and communication complexity. I also participated in the 2021 DIMACS REU program, where I worked with Prof. Eric Allender on metacomplexity (see my project blog). As of Fall 2021, I am applying to CS Ph.D. programs. Here is my CV.


Interests

I have broad interests in theoretical computer science, including in complexity, cryptography, and algorithms. Specific interests include using mathematical techniques (e.g. algebra, Boolean Fourier analysis) to answer questions about computation, and studying the role of interaction in computation (e.g. communication complexity, oracle complexity, zero-knowledge proofs).
