About Me

I'm interested in theoretical computer science. This fall, I'll be starting my Ph.D. in the Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University, where I'll be supported by an NSF GRFP fellowship. This summer, I'm continuing working on research and wrapping up some writing projects.

In Spring 2022, I graduated with an A.B. in Computer Science and Math from Harvard (Pforzheimer House '22), where I was fortunate to work with Prof. Madhu Sudan on streaming and communication complexity. Here is my CV.


Interests

I have broad theoretical interests, including in complexity, cryptography, algorithms, and combinatorics. One general theme of interest is applications of mathematical techniques, such as algebra, Boolean Fourier analysis, or spectral methods, to answer questions about computation. My undergrad research centered on the complexity of constraint satisfaction problems, and specifically on lower bounds and algorithms in the streaming setting.
